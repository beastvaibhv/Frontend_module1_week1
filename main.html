About Dehradun

Dehradun, the capital city of the northern Indian state of Uttarakhand, is a place of serene beauty and historical significance. Nestled in the picturesque Doon Valley and surrounded by the majestic Himalayan ranges, Dehradun offers a harmonious blend of natural splendor and cultural heritage.

One of the striking features of Dehradun is its pleasant climate throughout the year. Known for its mild winters and cool summers, the city attracts visitors looking for relief from the scorching heat of the plains during the summer months and those seeking a touch of winter charm during the colder season. The city's climate has earned it the nickname "The Adobe of Drona," a reference to the legendary Guru Dronacharya from the Indian epic, the Mahabharata.

Dehradun boasts lush greenery, with dense forests, rolling hills, and the meandering Ganges and Yamuna rivers. The nearby Rajaji National Park is a haven for wildlife enthusiasts and nature lovers, offering the chance to spot diverse species like elephants, tigers, leopards, and countless birds.

The city is also known for its prestigious educational institutions, including the iconic Doon School and the Forest Research Institute, which add to its intellectual and cultural richness. It is a hub for students and scholars, drawing young minds from across the country.

Dehradun's cultural heritage is deeply rooted in history. The city has witnessed the influence of various dynasties, including the Mauryas, Guptas, Mughals, and British, which is reflected in its architecture and traditions. The Robber's Cave, Sahastradhara, and Tapkeshwar Temple are just a few of the historical and natural attractions that enthrall tourists and locals alike.

The local cuisine of Dehradun is a delightful blend of North Indian and Garhwali flavors. From traditional dishes like Aloo Ke Gutke and Kumaoni Raita to mouthwatering street food like Chole Bhature and Momos, the city's culinary scene is a treat for the taste buds.

In recent years, Dehradun has also emerged as a hub for adventure enthusiasts, offering trekking, river rafting, paragliding, and camping opportunities in the nearby hills and rivers. With its natural beauty, pleasant climate, academic excellence, and cultural heritage, Dehradun holds a unique place in the hearts of those who visit and call it home. It is a city that effortlessly combines the old and the new, making it an enchanting destination for travelers seeking a rich cultural experience amidst breathtaking natural surroundings.

Attractions (Places to visit)

  • 1. Budhha Monastery

    The Buddha Temple, also known as Mindrolling Monastery, was founded in 1965 by His Eminence Kochen Rinpoche and a small group of other monks in Tibet to preserve and spread Buddhist teachings and culture. The Buddha Temple complex, one of the four Tibetan religious schools, was built in the Japanese architectural style and has a 103-foot-tall statue of the Lord Buddha. Nyingma is the name of the temple complex, whereas Kagyu, Sakya, and Geluk are the names of the schools housed there. There are statues of Buddha and Guru Padmasambhava on all five of the temple’s levels, which rise to a height of approximately 220 feet. It is one of the best places to see in Dehradun to experience and learn about Tibetan Culture. To get a panoramic view of Doon Valley, climb the wooden stairs to the fourth story and stand on the open circular observation platform. The temple is open all 7 days of the week.

  • 2. Shikhar fall

    Frequented by nature lovers and adventure seekers, Shikhar Falls is one of the most beautiful attractions in Dehradun. The waterfall is surrounded by mountainous terrain, so you need to go for a strenuous hike of about 1 km to reach the falls. And during this short hike, you will come across many wildflowers, shallow pools, pretty birds, and butterflies, which add to the beauty of the area. If you are a shutterbug, do remember to click some amazing photos here. And if you are a nature lover, you can simply sit back and enjoy the mesmerizing views of the surroundings.

  • 3. Guchu Pani/Robbers's Cave

    Also known as Guchu Pani, Robber’s Cave is a 600m-long cave with small waterfalls and a river flowing inside it. It is said that during the British times, robbers used to treat this cave as a hideout, and hence, the name. It is among the top 10 places to visit in Dehradun

  • 4. Guru Ram Rai Gurudwara

    popular attraction among devotees and tourists, Guru Ram Rai Gurudwara is visited by people from all states and religions. This illustrious holy place is also the oldest of its kind in Dehradun. The Gurudwara showcases a stunning blend of Indo-Islamic architecture with domes, minarets and murals adding a distinctive charm to it. When you step inside, you can see many religious inscriptions as well as murals and portraits of gods, goddesses, saints and sages on the walls. The Gurudwara premises has a huge pond as well.

  • 5. Paltan Bazar

    Whether you are looking to buy something or soak in local sights and sounds, a trip to Paltan Bazaar is a must when you are in Dehradun. This vibrant market stretches for more than 1.5 km near Dehradun Clock Tower and is popular as the main bazaar or shopping area in the city. Apart from apparel, appliances, handicrafts, and artworks, you can find the best local spices and confectionery items here. If you are looking for souvenirs, handwoven woolen garments and authentic Himalayan spices are some popular options.

  • 6. Malsi Deer Park

    Among the most popular Dehradun places to visit, Malsi Deer Park is a haven for wildlife lovers. This peaceful home to herds of deer is a favorite spot for family outings and school picnics. Besides the sleek spotted creatures, visitors can also capture several species of birds at Malsi Park.

  • 7. Forest Research Institute

    Are you interested in plants, birds, animals and other areas of forestry? Then make sure you visit Dehradun’s Forest Research Institute. This popular institute in the field of forestry research was established in 1906 and is known for its eye-catching colonial and Greco-Roman styles of architecture. Although an educational institution, it is open to visitors as well. It houses 6 museums, displaying the research on different areas in forestry. Surrounded by natural beauty, the institute attracts many nature lovers and picnickers as well.
